# Common Assumptions

Skill-bundled topic reference for the shared assumptions that underpin the `v8.2` root documentation tree.

Use this file after version routing when the user asks about regions, basins, GLUs, historical years, future years, or other cross-cutting assumptions that appear in multiple GCAM subsystems.

## Spatial Resolution
- Modern GCAM uses 32 geopolitical regions for the energy-economy frame.
- Water is represented with 235 major global river basins.
- Land is represented with hundreds of geographic land units, commonly summarized as 384 GLU-scale land subregions in the bundled modern line.

Important version cues:
- `v3.2` used an older 14-region framing.
- `v3.2` through `v4.4` still reflect an AEZ-oriented land history.
- `v5+` shifts modern land handling toward basin-linked GLU geography.
- `v8.x` includes Ukraine as an independent region in the modern region set.

## Temporal Resolution
- The data system can construct annual historical inputs beginning in 1971.
- The bundled modern baseline commonly calibrates to 1990, 2005, 2010, 2015, and 2021.
- Future periods in the bundled current baseline usually begin in 2025 and proceed in 5-year steps to 2100.

Version cues:
- `v8.0+` moves the model base year to 2021.
- Earlier modern releases use older final calibration years and should be interpreted through their version route file.

## Shared Choice Assumptions
- GCAM represents many economic choices using logit-style competition.
- Cost is usually the choice indicator in the energy system.
- Profit rate is the relevant indicator in the land system.
- Share weights calibrate historical structure and help phase in new technologies.

Open `choice_marketplace.md` when the user wants the logit mechanics or market interpretation.

## Shared Scenario Assumptions
- Population, GDP, productivity, technology costs, land and water coefficients, and policy settings define scenario conditions.
- GCAM results are internally consistent conditional scenarios, not forecasts.
- Historical calibration and future assumptions must remain structurally consistent across modules.

## Shared Data-System Assumptions
- A large fraction of GCAM XML inputs are generated by the data system rather than handwritten one-off files.
- Region, basin, GLU, and time-step assumptions are embedded both in model inputs and in data-system processing.

Open `data_system.md` when the user is asking where these assumptions are materialized into input XML.
